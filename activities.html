Indoors

My home based environment has many activities to help build the imagination of these young minds. Activities include building, drawing, painting, cooking, reading, and playing board games too. During meal times we all sit together helping to build key social skills.

We also have access to regular play groups to allow the children to play and interact with others, promoting diversity. We visit the baby library each week for books and sing-a-long too.

Outdoors

Weather allowing, we like to get out and about as much as possible. From simple strolls around the park, to visits to the seaside, and everything else in between, we always make the most of the good weather.

We don't let the bad weather stop us either! We'll still get wrapped up nice and warm and get outside to have some fun. Then when it snows, that's when the fun begins!

Adventures

There is always something planned to look forward to. Past trips have included picnics at the park, visits to Dudley Zoo & Castle, taking part in the big toddle, visiting local farms, and lots more.

We regularly meet up with other child minders for larger group activities too. Past events have included trips to Blackpool, the Sea Life Center, the Black Country Living Museum, and loads more!

Soft Play

At least twice a week - more if the weather is poor - we visit our local soft play center. This gives the children opportunity to mix with other children their age who they wouldn't normally see, and develop their social skills through interaction with others and learning to play and share.

All staff in the play center are fully trained in First Aid, Child Protection, Health & Safety, and Food Hygeine which makes it a fun and relaxed environment for the children to develop and play in.
